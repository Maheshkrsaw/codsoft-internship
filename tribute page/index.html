About Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh was born on September 28, 1907, in Punjab, India. He was a revolutionary freedom fighter who played a pivotal role in the Indian independence movement. Known for his courage, intellect, and dedication to the cause of freedom, Bhagat Singh became a symbol of resistance against British colonial rule.

His acts of defiance and sacrifice made him a hero among the Indian people, inspiring generations to come. His legacy continues to live on, representing the spirit of India's fight for independence.

Life and Achievements

1907

Birth

Bhagat Singh was born on September 28, 1907, in Banga, Punjab, British India.

1926

Formation of Naujawan Bharat Sabha

In 1926, Bhagat Singh founded the Naujawan Bharat Sabha, a revolutionary organization aimed at encouraging youth to fight for Indian independence.

1928

Assassination of Saunders

In 1928, Bhagat Singh and his associates avenged the death of Lala Lajpat Rai by killing British police officer J.P. Saunders.

1931

Martyrdom

On March 23, 1931, Bhagat Singh was executed by the British government at the age of 23, making him a martyr in India's struggle for independence.
